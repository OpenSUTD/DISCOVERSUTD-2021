About


DiscoverSUTD Human Library is an event where YOU can borrow "books" to have an informal conversation about anything; general knowledge regarding the Pillar/Programmes/Clusters, interests and experiences!

What are the books you might be wondering?

”Books” are our very own faculty professors and alumni who are able to share their certain knowledge and insights to YOU guys!

In intimate groups of 5, look forward to discuss some questions we prepared for sharing!

ASD

Prof Michael Budig
Michael Budig is an enthusiastic architect, teacher, and researcher, who is keen on examining the intersections between design and technology in architecture.
He investigates in renewable and regenerative architecture with the support of computational tools, and he is looking for innovative solutions to decrease the carbon footprint in the built environment.
He has 20 years of teaching experience around the world and more than 10 years of experience as practising architect with his own boutique firm.
He has been with SUTD for 7 years, where he is teaching the signature Freshmore course in Design Thinking and Innovation, Building Technologies in the first architecture core term and design studios.
Prof Christine Yogiaman
She had the pleasure to continually refine the teaching and learning of foundation design for architecture in the past 10 years. Starting from coordinating the graduate program core architecture studio sequence in Washington University in St Louis, USA; to incorporating digital design to core architecture studios in the American University of Sharjah, UAE. In SUTD, she had the pleasure to craft the foundation design curricula that premises on interdisciplinary design. Outside teaching, she direct Dynamic Assemblies Lab, a research lab in SUTD that explores design applications in biomaterial fabrication, Computational Fluid Dynamic simulation, and interactive architectural surfaces. She is also a partner at Yogiaman Tracy Design a design practice that focus on the utilization of digital techniques along with contextual influences to create culturally embedded, affective work.

CSD

Prof Tony Quek

Tony Q.S. Quek received the B.E. and M.E. degrees in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Tokyo Institute of Technology, Tokyo, Japan, respectively. At Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Cambridge, MA, he earned the Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. Currently, he is the Cheng Tsang Man Chair Professor with Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD). He also serves as the Head of ISTD Pillar, the Director of Future Communications Programme, and the Deputy Director of SUTD-ZJU IDEA. His current research topics include wireless communications and networking, big data processing, network intelligence, URLLC, and IoT. Dr. Quek has been actively involved in organizing and chairing and has served as a TPC member in numerous international conferences.
